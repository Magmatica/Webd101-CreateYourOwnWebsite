What is Sexuality?
When a person uses terms like bisexual or asexual, you may be startled thinking that it all involves sexual intercourse. Sexuality started as a means of telling people who you look at as a potential partner, romantic or otherwise, it is not your gender identity. Some people may use these terms to also stand as for romantic interest. Sexuality is a spectrum with numerous labels that people can choose to use. To start we need to look at the basics, which would be heterosexual/homosexual,bisexual, asexual, and graysexual. Heterosexual means that a person is attracted to those of the opposite gender identity whether that be women who like men, or men who like non-binary people, it means that the person likes another gender. Homosexual is when a person is attracted to those who share the same gender identity, like men who love men or genderfluid people who like genderfluid people. Bisexual is a term that means that a person is interested in more than one gender sometimes including their own gender. Asexual is a term that people use when they do not tend to feel sexual attraction or are not interested in it. Graysexual is a term for the gray area people can be in between sexual and asexual where sometimes they can lean more towards one side than the other.
